Bannerghatta National Park and Zoo

What is Bannerghatta Biological Park?

A large wildlife reserve located about 22 km south of Bengaluru, covering approximately 260 km², and includes a zoo, safari, butterfly park, rescue center, snake house, aquarium, and a pet corner.
Originally established in 1971–74 as a picnic corner and mini-zoo, it was later designated as a state biological park in 2002
Zoo (12 ha) – Home to various mammals, reptiles, birds such as tigers, lions, elephants, bears, king cobras, crocodiles, sloth bears, panthers, and more .
Butterfly Park – India’s first, opened in 2006, spanning about 7.5 acres, hosting over 20 butterfly species.
