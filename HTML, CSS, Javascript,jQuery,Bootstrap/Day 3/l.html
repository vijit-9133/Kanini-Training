Lalbagh Botanical Garden

What is Lalbagh Botanical Garden?

Lalbagh Botanical Garden is one of the most famous and well-maintained gardens in Bengaluru, Karnataka. Spread across 240 acres, it was originally commissioned by Hyder Ali and later expanded by his son, Tipu Sultan. The garden is home to over 1,800 species of plants, trees, and herbs.
At the heart of Lalbagh is the iconic glasshouse, inspired by London’s Crystal Palace. It hosts biannual flower shows that attract thousands of visitors from across the country. Beautiful walking paths and serene surroundings make it a popular spot for nature lovers and morning walkers alike.
The garden also features a centuries-old rock formation believed to be among the oldest on Earth. With scenic beauty, historical significance, and rich biodiversity, Lalbagh offers a peaceful retreat within the bustling city. It’s a must-visit for anyone interested in botany, history, or simply looking to enjoy a calm day outdoors.
